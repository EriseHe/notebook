### 3.1.1 Analytic Solution
Consider 1D Diffusion-convection problem with dirichlet boundary

$$
\left\{\begin{array}{l}
-\mu u^{\prime \prime}+\beta u^{\prime}=0, \quad \mu \geq 0 \\
u(0)=0 \quad \text { and } \quad u(1)=1
\end{array}\right.
$$

Then, the analytic solution is
$$
u = \frac{e^{\beta/\mu x}-1}{e^{\beta/\mu}-1}
$$
### 3.1.2 Finite Difference Discretization

For complex real-world problems, we discretize the continuous equation into a grid of points for computer to solve it algebraically.

By Taylor expansion on the neighboring points of $u_{i}$, we obtain
$$
\begin{aligned}
& u_{i+1}=u_i+h u_i^{\prime}+\frac{h^2}{2} u_i^{\prime \prime}+\frac{h^3}{6} u_i^{\prime \prime \prime}+\mathcal{O}\left(h^4\right) \\
& u_{i-1}=u_i-h u_i^{\prime}+\frac{h^2}{2} u_i^{\prime \prime}-\frac{h^3}{6} u_i^{\prime \prime \prime}+\mathcal{O}\left(h^4\right)
\end{aligned}
$$
Then, we first derive the second-derivative diffusion term:
$$
\begin{align}
u_{i+1} + u_{i-1}  & = 2u_{i} + h^{2}u''_{i}  + \mathcal{O}(h^4) \\ 
h^{2}u''_{i}  & =u_{i+1} -2u_{i} + u_{i-1} + \mathcal{O(h^4)}\\
u''_{i}  & =\frac{u_{i+1}-2u_{i} + u_{i+1} }{h^{2}}+\mathcal{O}(h^{2})
\end{align}
$$
we next derive the first-derivative convection term:
$$
\begin{aligned}
u_{i+1}-u_{i-1} & =2 h u_i^{\prime}+\frac{h^3}{3} u_i^{\prime \prime \prime}+\mathcal{O}\left(h^4\right) \\
2 h u_i^{\prime} & =u_{i+1}-u_{i-1}-\frac{h^3}{3} u_i^{\prime \prime \prime}+\mathcal{O}\left(h^4\right) \\
u_i^{\prime} & =\frac{u_{i+1}-u_{i-1}}{2 h}-\frac{h^2}{6} u_i^{\prime \prime \prime}+\mathcal{O}\left(h^3\right) \\
u_i^{\prime} & =\frac{u_{i+1}-u_{i-1}}{2 h}+\mathcal{O}\left(h^2\right)
\end{aligned}
$$
Finally, we obtain the discretization:
$$
\begin{aligned}
-\mu\underbrace{ \left(\frac{u_{i+1}-2 u_i+u_{i-1}}{h^{2}} \right) }_{ u^{\prime \prime}  \sim \mathcal{O}(h^{2})}+ \beta\underbrace{ \left(\frac{u_{i+1}-u_{i-1}}{2 h} \right)  }_{ u' \sim\mathcal{O}(h^{2})}& = 0 
\end{aligned}
$$

### 3.1.2 Stability and physical reliability

To solve this for all grid points, we group the coefficients associated with each specific point ($u_{i+1}, u_i, u_{i-1}$) from the last formula:

$$
\begin{align}
\left(-\frac{\mu}{h^2} u_{i+1}+\frac{2 \mu}{h^2} u_i-\frac{\mu}{h^2} u_{i-1}\right)+\left(\frac{\beta}{2 h} u_{i+1}-\frac{\beta}{2 h} u_{i-1}\right) & =0\\
\underbrace{\left(\frac{\mu}{h^2}-\frac{\beta}{2 h}\right)}_a u_{i+1}-\underbrace{\frac{2 \mu}{h^2}}_b u_i+\underbrace{\left(\frac{\mu}{h^2}+\frac{\beta}{2 h}\right)}_c u_{i-1} & =0
\end{align}
$$

with boundary conditions $u_0=0$ and ${} u_N=1$. 

>this turns into a second-order difference equation, which allows us to find the "roots" of the characteristic equation to see if solution stays smooth or oscillate.

---

We then have a characteristic equation, 
$$
\begin{align}
 &   a \rho^2+b \rho+c=0\\
 & a=\frac{\mu}{h^2}-\frac{\beta}{2 h}, \quad b=-\frac{2 \mu}{h^2}, \quad c=\frac{\mu}{h^2}+\frac{\beta}{2 h}
\end{align}
$$
where we obtain the solution as
$$
\rho_1=1, \quad \rho_2=\frac{c}{a}=\frac{\mu / h^2+\beta /(2 h)}{\mu / h^2-\beta /(2 h)}=\frac{1+\frac{\beta h}{2 \mu}}{1-\frac{\beta h}{2 \mu}}
$$
The behavior of this numerical solution depends the sign of $\rho$. To ensure it is **physically realistic** (smooth), we observe
$$
\begin{aligned}
& \frac{\beta h}{2 \mu}<1 \Longrightarrow \rho_2>0 \quad \Longrightarrow\;\; \text { no sign-flip oscillation } \\
& \frac{\beta h}{2 \mu}>1 \Longrightarrow \rho_2<0 \quad \Longrightarrow \;\;\text { zig-zag oscillation. }
\end{aligned}
$$
From here, we obtain the Pèclet Number $\left(\mathbb{P}_e\right)$ by this condition:

> [!definition] Péclet number
> For the 1D convection-diffusion problem, $-\mu u''+\beta u'=0$, the **Cell Péclet number** is defined by
> $$
> \mathbb{P}_e=\frac{|\beta|\,h}{2\mu}
> $$

> [!remark]
> Péclet number measures the relative strength of **convection** to **diffusion** on one mesh cell:
> $$
\begin{align}
\mathbb{P}_e & \ll 1 \;\Rightarrow\; \text{diffusion-dominated}\\
\mathbb{P}_e & \gg 1 \;\Rightarrow\; \text{convection-dominated}
\end{align}
> $$
>
> For the centered finite-difference scheme, the standard non-oscillation condition is
> $$
> \mathbb{P}_e\le 1.
> $$


Problem: If $\frac{\mu}{\beta}=10^{-6}$, since we require step size:
$$
\frac{\beta h}{2 \mu}<1 \Longrightarrow h<\frac{2 \mu}{\beta} 
$$
then we would need over 1 million points... 

## 3.1.3 Upwind Scheme

> The centered difference scheme forces us to use a tiny grid size. Can we have a better scheme?

Yes, the famous workaround is **upwind scheme** that breaks the symmetry.

> [!remark]
> Information travels with the wind.

If the wind is blowing from left to right ($\beta > 0$), what happens at point $i$ is mostly influenced by what is coming from behind it (point $i-1$). So, we approximate the advection term using only a backward difference:$$\boxed{\beta u' \approx \beta \frac{u_i - u_{i-1}}{h}}$$

1. accuracy drops from $\mathcal{O}(h^2)$ to $\mathcal{O}(h)$
2. mathematically sacrifice, but *physically consistent

> [!theorem]
> **upwind scheme** guarantees that the solution never oscillate, regardless of grid size $h$ or wind speed $\beta$.

Proof. 

**PART 1**. We start with the definition of the Upwind derivative 
$$
\begin{align}
\beta  \frac{{u_{i}-u_{i-1}}}{h }  & = \frac{\beta}{2h}(2u_{i}-2u_{i-1}) \\
 &   =   \frac{\beta}{2h}(u_{i+1}-u_{i-1}-u_{i-1}+2u_{i}-u_{i-1}) \\
  &  = \beta  \underbrace{ \frac{{u_{i+1}-u_{i-1}}}{2h}  }_{ u'_{\text{center}} }- \frac{\beta h}{2} \underbrace{ \left( \frac{{u_{i-1} -2u_{i}+u_{i-1}}}{h^2} \right) }_{ u'' } \\ 
\end{align}
$$
That is,
$$
\beta u'_{\text{\scriptsize UPW}}= \beta u'_{\text{center}} - \frac{\beta h}{2}u''
$$
**PART 2. ** Next, we plug back to the original problem

$$
\begin{align}
  -\mu u''  &     + \beta u'_{\text{\scriptsize UPW}}   &    = 0 \\
  -\mu u'' &   + \beta  \left( u'_{\text{center}} - \frac{ h}{2}u'' \right)   &     =0     \\
  -\left( \mu +\frac{\beta h}{2} \right)u'' &   + \beta u'_{\text{center}}   &     =0 
\end{align}
$$
Here, recall that 
$$
\mathbb{P}_{e} = \frac{\beta h}{2\mu} \quad \Longrightarrow \quad\mu\mathbb{P}_{e} = \frac{\beta h}{2}
$$
So substitute into, we have 

$$
\begin{align}
  -\left( \mu +\mu \mathbb{P}_{e} \right)u'' + \beta u'_{\text{center}}   &    =0  \\
  -\underbrace{ \mu\left( 1 + \mathbb{P}_{e} \right) }_{ \mu' }u'' + \beta u'_{\text{center}}  & =0
\end{align}
$$
Hence, the Peclet number of the problems under upwind scheme becomes:
$$
\mathbb{P}^{\text{\scriptsize UPW}}_{e}=\mathbb{P}^{*}_{e} = \frac{\beta h}{2\mu^{*}}= \frac{\beta h}{2\mu(1+\mathbb{P}_{e})} = \frac{\mathbb{P}_{e}}{1+\mathbb{P}_{e}}<1
$$
since $\mathbb{P}_{e } > 0$ is always true.
