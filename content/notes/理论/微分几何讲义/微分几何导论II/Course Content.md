
## MATH 546: SPRING 2026 SUMMARY OF COURSE CONTENTS

Below, I summarize the topics covered in our class and provide some references.

Lecture 1: Radial sound speed. We solved the travel time tomography problem for radially symmetric metrics under the Herglotz condition. See Chapter 2 of [5].

Lecture 2: Differential forms. We discussed the construction of differential forms on smooth manifolds. See Section V.5, V. 6 of [2].

Lecture 3: Symplectic geometry. We discussed the construction of exterior derivative. See Section V. 8 of [2]. Then we discussed the symplectic structure and Hamilton vector fields etc. See Chapter 3 of [3] or [1].

Lecture 4: Geometric preliminaries. First, we discussed smooth manifolds with boundary, see Section VI. 4 of [2]. Then we talked about non-trapping and strict convexity, see Section 3.1 of [5]. Finally, we discussed exit time and scattering relation, see Section 3.2, 3.3 of [5].

Lecture 5: Boundary determination. We formulated the boundary rigidity problem and showed the determination of metrics at the boundary. See Section 11.111.3 of [5].

Lecture 6: Integration on manifold. We discussed the construction of Riemann integral on smooth manifolds. Also, we talked about orientation, volume element and Stokes theorem. See V.7, VI.1, VI.2, VI. 5 of [2].

Lecture 7: Rigidity in a conformal class. First, we proved Santaló's formula. Then we used it to show the rigidity in a conformal class. See Section 3.6.2 and 11.4 of [5].

Lecture 8: Linearization and geodesic ray transform. From the linearization of the boundary distance function, we derived the geodesic ray transform for two tensors. Then we discussed the injectivity of the transform for the Euclidean metric
on functions. Finally, we reduced the injectivity question to the study of transport equations. See Chapter 1 of [6] and Section 4.1-4.2 of [5].

Lecture 9: Vector fields on SM. See Section 3.5-3.6 of [5].
Lecture 10: Pestov identity. We proved the Pestov identity and used it to show the injectivity and stability of the geodesic ray transform for non-positively curved metrics. See Section 4.3-4.5 of [5].

Lecture 11: Injectivity for simple metrics. We proved the injectivity of the geodesic ray transform for simple metrics. Before that, we discussed Jacobi field, conjugate points, index form etc. See Section 3.7 and 4.4, 4.6 of [5] and [4].
