## Problem 2.35

Find the energy stored in a uniformly charged solid sphere of radius $R$ and charge $q$. Do it three different ways:

### (a) Use Eq. 2.43. You found the potential in Prob. 2.22.

Solution. using $$\boxed{W = \frac{1}{2} \int \rho V d\tau}$$
To get $V$, use Gauss' Law to get $\vec{E}$ first:

outside:
$$
\begin{aligned}
E_{out} \cdot 4\pi r_{out}^2 &= \frac{Q_{en}}{\epsilon_0} = \frac{q}{\epsilon_0} \\
\Rightarrow \vec{E}_{out}(\vec{r}) &= \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q}{r^2} \hat{r}
\end{aligned}
$$

inside:
$$
\begin{aligned}
E_{in} \cdot 4\pi r_{in}^2 &= \frac{Q_{en}}{\epsilon_0} = \frac{q}{\epsilon_0} \frac{\frac{4}{3}\pi r_{in}^3}{\frac{4}{3}\pi R^3} \\
\Rightarrow \vec{E}_{in} (\vec{r}) &= \frac{q}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{\vec{r}}{R^3}
\end{aligned}
$$

Then, $V(\vec{r})= -\int_{\infty}^{\vec{r}} \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l}$, only needed for $r < R$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\Rightarrow V(\vec{r}) &= - \int_{\infty}^{R} E_{out} \cdot dr - \int_{R}^{r} E_{in} \cdot dr \\
&= - \frac{q}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \left\{ \int_{\infty}^{R} \frac{dr}{r^2} + \frac{1}{R^3} \int_{R}^{r} r dr \right\} \\
&= - \frac{q}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \left\{ -\frac{1}{R} + \frac{1}{2R^3} (r^2 - R^2) \right\} \\
&= \frac{3}{8\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q}{R} \left(1 - \frac{r^2}{3R^2}\right)
\end{aligned}
$$

Finally,
$$
\begin{aligned}
W &= \frac{1}{2} \int_{sph} \rho V d\tau \\
&= \frac{q}{\frac{8}{3}\pi R^3} \cdot \frac{3}{8\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q}{R} 4\pi \int_0^R \left(1 - \frac{r^2}{3R^2}\right) r^2 dr \\
&\quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \underbrace{\quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad}_{\frac{1}{3}R^3 - \frac{1}{15}R^3 = \frac{4R^3}{15}} \\
\Rightarrow W &= \frac{3}{20\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q^2}{R} //
\end{aligned}
$$
